01 — Overview

Simplifying the Complex

Nebusis® is an AI-powered SaaS platform designed to revolutionize how organizations manage ISO compliance. Traditional compliance management was plagued by manual processes, fragmented systems, and poor user experiences that made simple tasks unnecessarily complex.

My role encompassed product management, UI/UX design, and project management, guiding the platform from concept through launch. The journey involved navigating a major crisis, rebuilding from scratch, and ultimately delivering a solution that transforms compliance from burden to strategic advantage.

Project Highlights

  • Led complete redesign of compliance management platform serving enterprise clients
  • Recovered from critical website failure by rebuilding entire system in compressed timeframe
  • Implemented mobile-first design approach using Google UX principles
  • Created comprehensive design system with 50+ custom assets and vectors
  • Delivered 4 distinct landing pages with integrated AI policy generation
02 — The Challenge

Understanding the Problem

ISO compliance systems were stuck in the past, creating friction rather than facilitating progress.

The Problem

Organizations struggled with outdated ISO management systems that required extensive manual work, created compliance gaps, and provided frustrating user experiences. Teams spent more time fighting the system than improving actual compliance.

  • Manual data entry and document management
  • No real-time visibility into compliance status
  • Inconsistent documentation across teams
  • Poor mobile experience for field workers
  • Steep learning curve for new users

The Solution

Nebusis® transforms compliance management through intelligent automation, intuitive design, and real-time analytics. We made the complex simple and the tedious automated.

  • AI-powered policy generation and management
  • Real-time dashboards and compliance tracking
  • Mobile-first responsive design
  • Centralized documentation system
  • Intuitive workflows requiring minimal training

Before

The original interface was cluttered, difficult to navigate, and not optimized for modern workflows.

After

Clean, modern interface with clear information hierarchy and intuitive navigation.

03 — Research & Discovery

Understanding Users & Context

User Research

I began by conducting extensive user research with compliance managers, auditors, and field technicians. Through interviews and observational studies, I identified key pain points and workflow inefficiencies.

Key Findings

  • Users spent 60% of time on administrative tasks vs. strategic work
  • Mobile access was critical but poorly supported
  • Learning curve prevented adoption across teams
  • Real-time visibility was demanded but unavailable

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed competing ISO management platforms, identifying gaps in the market and opportunities for differentiation. Most solutions prioritized features over usability, creating opportunities for a design-led approach.

Early website fixes and concepts exploring information architecture.

04 — Design Process

From Crisis to Success

A complete rebuild that became an opportunity for transformation

Initial Assessment & Wireframing

My involvement began with corrections to ISO course materials, which led to identifying major usability issues in the existing website. I applied Google UX principles to restructure the information architecture, creating mobile-first wireframes that prioritized user task flows.

The wireframing phase focused on simplifying complex workflows, reducing cognitive load, and ensuring responsive design patterns would work seamlessly across devices.

Architecture & User Flows

I mapped out comprehensive user flows for each persona, identifying opportunities to eliminate steps and reduce friction. The architecture needed to accommodate complex compliance requirements while remaining intuitive for non-technical users.

This phase involved extensive prototyping and testing with stakeholders to validate assumptions and refine the approach before investing in high-fidelity designs.

Crisis Management & Pivot

During critical client onboarding, the website crashed with no backup available. This crisis became a catalyst for improvement. Using saved Figma assets, I rapidly rebuilt the entire system, implementing a more robust design system and establishing better version control processes.

The rebuild allowed us to address technical debt and implement improvements that would have been difficult to justify otherwise. What seemed like a disaster became an opportunity to start fresh with lessons learned.

Design System & Launch

I developed a comprehensive design system including custom vectors, consistent typography, color systems, and component libraries. The system needed to support both light and dark modes while maintaining brand consistency across all touchpoints.

Successfully launched 4 distinct landing pages, each optimized for different user segments and compliance standards. Integrated AI-powered policy generation made complex compliance tasks as simple as filling out a form.

06 — Key Learnings

Insights & Takeaways

Always Have Backups

The crisis taught me the critical importance of maintaining multiple backups and implementing robust version control systems for all project assets. Now I maintain redundant backups across cloud storage, local drives, and Git repositories.

Mobile-First Design

Starting with mobile constraints leads to cleaner, more focused designs that scale beautifully to desktop. This approach forces prioritization and eliminates unnecessary complexity from the start.

User-Centered Complexity

Complex B2B systems require even more attention to user experience than consumer products. Simplicity becomes a competitive advantage when dealing with sophisticated workflows and technical requirements.
