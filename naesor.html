Naesor

Crafting a seamless music experience for a Google challenge.

My Role
UI/UX Designer, Product Designer
Timeline
Google UX Design Challenge
Platform
Mobile App (iOS/Android)
Industry
Music Streaming, Digital Content
01 — Overview

The Design Challenge

Designing an intuitive music album app that enhances user engagement and discovery, as part of a Google UX Design challenge.

The Problem

Existing music apps often suffer from cluttered interfaces, complex navigation, and a lack of personalized discovery, leading to user frustration and missed opportunities for artists.

Our Solution

Naesor was conceived as a clean, user-centric album app focusing on seamless navigation, personalized content, and an engaging visual experience, making music discovery a joy.

02 — Process

The Design Journey: From Concept to Interactive Prototype

A user-centered approach to crafting a seamless and engaging music experience.

User Research & Empathy Mapping

Began by understanding target users' needs, pain points, and desires in music consumption. Focused on how users discover, organize, and interact with their favorite albums and artists.

1
2

Information Architecture & Wireframing

Structured the app's content logically, creating intuitive navigation paths. Developed low-fidelity wireframes to define layout and functionality, ensuring a clear user flow.

Prototyping & Interaction Design

Built interactive prototypes to simulate the user experience, allowing for early testing and iteration. Focused on smooth transitions, responsive elements, and engaging micro-interactions.

3
4

Visual Design & High-Fidelity Mockups

Translated wireframes into polished, high-fidelity designs, applying a modern aesthetic with a focus on album art and readability. Ensured visual consistency across all screens.

Key Feature Showcase: Seamless Purchasing

Designed a streamlined purchase flow for albums and merchandise, minimizing steps and providing clear feedback to the user. This included intuitive cart management and secure checkout.

5
03 — Results

Outcome & Impact

The Naesor project successfully demonstrated a user-centered approach to mobile app design, resulting in a highly intuitive and visually appealing music experience.

Intuitive Navigation
Engaging Visuals
Streamlined Purchase Flow
High User Satisfaction (Conceptual)
04 — Learnings

Key Takeaways

User-Centric Design

Prioritizing user needs from discovery to delivery ensures a product that truly resonates and provides value.

Iterative Prototyping

Rapid prototyping and testing are crucial for refining interactions and identifying usability issues early in the design process.

Visual Storytelling

Effective use of high-fidelity mockups and clear visual hierarchy is key to communicating the product's value and aesthetic.
